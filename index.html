My personal journey into aviation began in March 2011, when I joined Lufthansa German Airlines as a flight attendant on the Airbus fleet. This was my first step into professional aviation — and from that moment on, my goal was clear: becoming an airline pilot.

2005

First Career

After completing my German A-levels, I trained as a paramedic and worked in emergency medical services. Alongside this, I was self-employed for several years as a presenter and host, in web and media design, and in music production — early years defined by curiosity and breadth.

2011

Entry into Aviation

In March 2011, I joined Lufthansa German Airlines as a flight attendant on the Airbus fleet — my first step into professional aviation. The decision was made: I wanted to fly from the front.

Flight Training
2018

Pilot Academy

In February 2018, I started my integrated ATPL(A) training at the Lufthansa Aviation Training Pilot Academy. During training, I successfully passed the Lufthansa Group pilot selection.

2019

Graduation

Graduated in December 2019 with CPL(A), MCC, and ATPL(A) theory — fully prepared for line operations on a commercial aircraft.

Airline Operations
2020

First Officer · Eurowings

Today I am a First Officer on the Airbus A320 family with Eurowings, a position I have proudly held for more than three years. In addition to my airline duties, I am a qualified flight instructor — combining operational flying with teaching and mentoring aspiring pilots.

Now

Academia & Beyond

Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Aviation Business & Piloting, deepening my understanding of airline operations, management, and the future role of data and AI in aviation. Based in Berlin — always open for new challenges.
